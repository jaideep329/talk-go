Conclusion

The effect is present in both deployments, but it is much more prevalent and much more severe in the new Go voice worker. The old Python worker has a non-trivial 60-90 second tail, but almost no rows exceed 120 seconds and none exceed 300 seconds. The Go worker has a materially larger tail: 17.6% over 120 seconds, 10.3% over 180 seconds, and 11 rows over 300 seconds.

Interpretation

  • Not Go container startup: Go scheduled-to-started p95 is 2s and max is 7s. The slow rows waited before scheduling.
  • Not only scheduler capacity: FailedScheduling appears in both deployments at similar pod-level rates: old at least 3.2%, Go 2.4%. It is real but does not explain the Go-only long tail.
  • Autoscaler timing exists in both: Old Python also has HPA below/churn and late-scale-up cases in its tail, so this mechanism is not unique to Go.
  • Go is the outlier: Go has 19.2x the >=120s rate, 181.7x the >=180s rate, and rows over 300 seconds while old has none in this export.
